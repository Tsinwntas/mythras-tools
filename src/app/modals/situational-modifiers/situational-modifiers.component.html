Weapon Size Difference

Each participant needs to fight at their weapon's size distance otherwise penalties are applied.

Reach difference of two or more steps for range to make effect.

If the two opponents are at the distance of the longer weapon then the shorter weapon carrier can only target the weapon of their opponent.

If the two opponents are at the distance of the shorter weapon then:

  • the longer weapon damage becomes a max of 1D3+1 Damage.
  • the longer weapon size is considered equal to the smaller weapon size
  • the longer weapon cannot be used to parry

Climbing

A climbing character has all of his combat skills capped by the value of his Athletics Skill. Bipedal Characters are limited to fighting with a single limb, requiring the other three to remain clinging to the wertiginous surface they are scaling.

Swimming

A swimming character has all of his compbat skills capped by the calue of his Swim Skill. Since water (and other fluids) impose significant drag, only thrusting weapons are effective whilst submerged, and even those are reduced to half damage. Other weapons are slowed to the point of impotence. Unarmed combat attacks which involve bitting, strangulation or ramming are unafffected.

Unfamiliar Weapons

The penalty imposed by use of an unfamiliar weapon cannot reduce the user's skill below the default level of STR+DEX.
