The character can attempt to close on, or retreat from, an opponent; changing the range at which he is fighting to take best advantage of his weapon's Reach or disengage from engagement entirely.

Reach difference of two or more steps for range to make effect.

Oppose Closing/Opening Range:

Either keep the range

Evade skills in an Opposed test.

  • If initiator of the Change Range wins then he can close/open the range to a desired distance
  • If the opponent wins then the range is maintained

Or Attack the changing character

Make an Opposed Roll Combat Skill vs the initiators' Evade

  • If the opponent wins, he strikes the character, and any difference in Level of Success results in Special Effects
  • The initiator changes the distance

Results:

Beyond range

Can withdraw without Special Effect

Longer Reach

  • If fighting at the Reach of the longer weapon, then the user of the shorter weapon cannot directly Attack the wielder of the longer. They are still considered to be engaged.
  • User of the shorter weapon can Attack the longer weapon, try to close the range between them, or hope to win an applicable Special Effect.

Shorter Reach

  • Longer weapon cannot Parry
  • The longer weapon's Size is reduced as many steps as the difference between the two weapons' Reach
  • Longer weapon can only do at most 1D3+1 damage
  • Getting within Shorter Reach of Natural Weapons does not have the above limitations (Damage is normal, Can Parry, No reduction of Size)
